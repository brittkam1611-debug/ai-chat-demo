Scene 6 — Ethics & Judgment

What Would You Do?

The Situation
Marcus just got off the phone with a member going through a difficult divorce. The member is emotional and confused about their legal benefits. Marcus wants to send a helpful, warm follow-up email — but he's behind on 12 other cases and has 20 minutes left in his day. He considers using AI to write the email.
BEST APPROACH
B. Use AI with a generic prompt, then personalize YOUR CHOICE
This is the ideal blend. AI handles the structural heavy lifting — drafting clear, professional language quickly — while Marcus keeps personal details out of the tool and adds the human touch where it matters most. It's fast AND careful AND warm. This is what skilled AI use looks like.
ACCEPTABLE — WITH CAVEATS
C. Skip AI — write it fully yourself YOUR CHOICE
Totally valid. For high-sensitivity situations, a fully human response is sometimes the right call — and there's no data risk. The only downside is time. As you build confidence with AI, you'll find ways to let it help on the structural parts while keeping your voice at the center.
ACCEPTABLE — BUT RISKY
D. Adapt a previous email YOUR CHOICE
Using a previous email as a template is a reasonable workaround — but it only works if you edit it carefully. Copy-paste errors are common under pressure, and a mismatched detail (wrong name, wrong issue) can erode member trust fast. If you go this route, slow down and read it through before sending.
PROBLEMATIC
A. Paste member details directly into ChatGPT and send as-is YOUR CHOICE
Two issues: First, pasting a member's name, case details, and emotional state into an external AI tool violates data privacy — that information is now outside your control. Second, sending without review risks sending generic, emotionally flat text to someone who needs genuine support. Speed at this cost isn't worth it.
