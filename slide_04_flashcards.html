Scene 2 — What AI Can & Can't Do

Myth vs. Fact

Click each card to reveal the truth. Flip all 6 to continue.

MYTH
AI knows everything and is always right. If it sounds confident, it must be accurate.
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
AI generates plausible text — it doesn't "know" facts. It hallucinates, makes things up confidently, and has no awareness of what's true. Always verify important outputs.
MYTH
AI will replace my job. There's no point in learning skills anymore.
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
AI replaces tasks, not roles. People who use AI effectively outperform those who don't — making you more valuable, not obsolete. The skill now is knowing how to work with it.
MYTH
Better prompts = more words. The longer and more detailed, the better.
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
Clarity beats length. A focused prompt with context, a clear role, and a specific output format outperforms a rambling paragraph every time. The CRAFT framework teaches this exactly.
MYTH
AI is private and secure by default. What I type in stays between me and the tool.
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
Most AI tools are NOT confidential environments. Input can be logged, used for training, or seen by vendors. Never enter PII, member data, passwords, or proprietary information.
MYTH
"If AI wrote it, it's not really my work — so I'm off the hook if it's wrong."
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
You own everything you send with your name on it. AI is a tool, not an excuse. If you submit AI output without reviewing it, the errors — and the consequences — are yours.
MYTH
Using AI to write member communications saves time with no downside.
Click to reveal the fact
FACT
Speed has a cost if you're not careful. AI doesn't know your member's situation, tone preferences, or emotional state. Unreviewed AI output can feel cold, generic, or factually wrong to the person receiving it.
